about me

I'm a first-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of California, Berkeley. I work with Professor Pieter Abbeel and Professor Sergey Levine in the Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research (BAIR) Lab.

My research focuses on approaches to, algorithms for, and applications of learning control for real-world robotic systems. Currently I am exploring deep reinforcement learning methods for control of tensegrity robots. I am also interested in the general techniques and principles of transfer learning, mainly from simulation to real-world but also across different robots and different environments.

During my time as an undergraduate in the same institution, I was a part of various activities involving education and music. I was an undergraduate student instructor (UGSI, or TA) for CS 189/289A, CS 188, and CS 61A. I served as the President of The Music Connection, a club that provides free music tutoring to children in the Berkeley community. In some semesters, I played violin in the UC Berkeley Symphony Orchestra. My non-academic interests include running (every now and then), binge watching shows on Netflix, and every type of Asian food.
