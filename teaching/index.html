teaching

I was an undergraduate student instructor (UGSI, or TA) for CS 189/289A, the machine learning course at Berkeley, for the Spring 2016 semester. In the Fall 2015 semester, I was a UGSI for CS 188, the introductory artificial intelligence course. Before that, I was a UGSI for CS 61A, the introductory computer science course, for three semesters. All of these courses are extremely popular, and have seen enrollment grow exponentially in recent years. Teaching was a major facet of my time as an undergraduate, and my instructor ratings can be found here.

During my time teaching CS 61A, I was involved in several parts of the course beyond just TAing. Most notably, I developed a new project for the course, and I was the lead instructor with Brian Hou for the Summer 2016 semester, where I had the opportunity to give lectures for over 400 students and mentor a new generation of TAs. All resources from the Summer 2016 semester, including lecture slides and assignments, can be found from the course website.

projects

Brian Hou, Marvin Zhang, and John DeNero. Restaurant Recommendations. SIGCSE Nifty Assignments, 2016.

This is a project that I developed along with fellow UGSI Brian Hou and Professor John DeNero. In this project, students construct a visualization of restaurant ratings for restaurants in the Berkeley area utilizing the Yelp Academic Dataset, using both existing user ratings as well as predicting ratings by analyzing user preferences. This project teaches concepts such as data abstraction, lists, and dictionaries, and also serves as an introduction into the field of machine learning. This project was featured as a part of SIGCSE Nifty Assignments.
